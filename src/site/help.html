Set Cache in Your Browser:

Using Netscape 4.0 - go to your browser's top menu - click on - Edit > Preferences > Advanced, click on the + sign next to Advanced > Cache - set both "Memory Cache" and "Disk Cache" to "0", then check "Every time" under "Document in cache is compared to document on network"

Using IE, Microsoft's Internet Explorer 4.0 - go to your browser's top menu - click on - View > Internet Options > Security - under "Set the security level for this zone", check Medium [more secure]

Configure Your Experience!

You may choose to "Configure" any room by choosing to ignore certain people and only see messages posted from those you choose. To select a group of friends in a room, simply check the Private Message box next to their names. Check the IGNORE box next to everyone's name that you do not wish to share a conversation with. To keep the boxes checked and carry on a conversion with the same people in private, be sure to click on the "Speak" button after you check the Private message box. If you click on "Get New Messages" or use the Express Public Message Box, your selections will not be stored.

Time Out/Ghosted:

There is a ten minute grace period for inactivity, then your handle will be removed from the room to make space for another person.
Occasionally we will restart the server, when we do, you will be ejected from the room:-(
The good news is that you will be brought right to the entry page of the room you were in, and your handle will already be in the text area! All you have to do to re enter is hit the "enter" button.

Microsoft Browsers:

Room Creator FAQ:

How do I Edit My Private Room?

Using the Creator to create a private room, and having Admin privileges to edit the room in the future:

When you create a new private room using the Creator, it is important to know that when you are finished creating the room and you click on the "Create My Chatroom" button, you are doing two things. Essentially you are creating a new chat room, also you are logging into a chat room.

If the little gray login box pops up and asks you for a "User Name" and "Password", then it is like you are logging into a chat room for the first time.

Your login info is the key; the program will recognize you as the Admin to that room. In the future it will allow you to come back in and edit the room... so every time after that you must log in using the exact same "User Name" and "Password" (CASE SENSITIVE) as you did when you created the room, if you want Admin privileges to edit the room in the future. Which means seeing the "Edit" button inside the room.

If you logged in to a another chat room prior to creating a room, then your login info, "User Name" and "Password" are the key here as well. The same rules apply to the program recognizing you as the Creator/Editor of that room.

I Created another Room by accident, Why?

When you Edit your room and you change the Name of the Room to something other than the Original Name, then the program creates another New Room. This is not true for sites using their own database of registered users.
